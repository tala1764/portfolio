Game Development


Description:

A game developer is a person who works and develops the features inside a game, the developers use the designers' ideas, storyboards, and designs as blueprints throughout the development process of the game.


Required Skills:


  • Unity- a cross-platform game engine to develop 2D and 3D games. Unity is particularly popular for indie and mobile games. Free for students and personal use, able to start creating games (and gaining experience) before getting hired.
  • Unreal Engine- while initially developed for first-person shooters, has grown into a 3D creation platform used for games across a range of platforms. Unreal uses C++ as its programming language.
  • C# (pronounced C-sharp)- is a programming language frequently used for game development and mobile applications, particularly with the Unity engine.
  • C++ Is a general-purpose programming language often used to develop not only games but also operating systems and applications as well.


Salary:

-The average base pay starts from $74,838 ( as of October 22)

Why It's My Choice?..

The reason why this career is my choice goes back to my passion and love for videogames and working individually as well. I consider gaming as my number 1 hobby, a strong love for such a job!

Education Needed:

-Many game development and publishing companies look for candidates with a bachelor's degree in computer science or related field. Some universities have started offering degrees or minors in game design and interactive entertainment that are more specifically targeted toward the video game industry.
